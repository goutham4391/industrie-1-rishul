Recent News

Graduate student’s multidisciplinary engineering experience leads to opportunities

Whitney Mantooth, a health and safety manager for Koch Industries, realized her desire to pursue engineering and joined the doctoral program in the Department of Multidisciplinary Engineering at Texas A&M University to further her career.

Automatic machines’ precision increased by mathematical models

Dr. Aldo J. Munoz-Vazquez is leading a study that uses sophisticated mathematics to predict the behavior of a larger class of dynamical systems more accurately. This research can help automatic machines be more efficient and precise.

Preventing hydrocarbon buildup in subsea systems

Dr. Keshawa Shukla investigates cooldown times for the operation of subsea pipe-in-pipe flowline systems. By doing so, he hopes to prevent the solid wax deposition of hydrocarbons and ultimately streamline the production process.
